# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Positioning Statement

**For**: Finance teams and tech leads at 5–500 person companies
**Who need**: Visibility into AI tool spend and portfolio optimization
**The AI Spend Audit**: Is a free, no-login audit tool
**That**: Identifies cost savings and tool redundancies in seconds
**Unlike**: Spreadsheet audits and procurement platforms
**Because**: It's fast, shareable, and respects privacy

---

## Target Customer Personas

### Persona 1: Finance Manager (Primary)

**Name**: Jamie, Finance Manager at 50-person SaaS startup

**Background**:
- Responsible for controlling spend across engineering + operations
- Recently tasked with auditing "random AI tool charges"
- Uses spreadsheets + Expensify to track spend
- Frustrated that engineering teams don't coordinate tool purchases

**Pain**:
- "We have ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, some custom API spend... I have no idea how much we're actually spending"
- "Every time I ask what we're using, I get a different answer"
- "Finance leadership is pressuring me to show ROI on AI tools"

**Motivation**:
- Wants credible, fast audit to present to leadership
- Wants to stop questions about AI spend ("Here's an audit")
- Wants to identify quick wins ($5K+ savings)

**Buying behavior**:
- Tries free tools first
- Shares internally before considering paid
- Needs business case + ROI to justify premium tool

### Persona 2: Engineering Lead (Secondary)

**Name**: Alex, VP Engineering at 100-person company

**Background**:
- Responsible for engineering tool stack
- Didn't know other teams were using Claude/ChatGPT
- Wants to optimize team but doesn't know where to start

**Pain**:
- "We have overlap — 3 teams using ChatGPT, 2 using Claude, 1 using custom"
- "Can't convince teams to consolidate without data"
- "Need clear recommendations to present to other engineering leads"

**Motivation**:
- Wants data to back consolidation decision
- Wants to show engineering discipline to leadership
- Wants to free up budget for other investments

**Buying behavior**:
- Shares with peers ("Hey, this tool exists")
- Unlikely to pay (engineers don't like tools)
- Becomes evangelist if tool is useful

### Persona 3: CISO/Security Lead (Tertiary)

**Name**: Sam, CISO at 200-person company

**Background**:
- Concerned about shadow IT + unapproved tools
- Needs inventory of AI tools + access
- Wants audit trail + compliance

**Pain**:
- "I don't know what AI tools we're using or who has access"
- "We need to audit third-party AI access before SOC 2 compliance"
- "Need a quick report to share with auditors"

**Motivation**:
- Wants comprehensive tool inventory
- Wants compliance documentation
- Wants to reduce security risk from unauthorized tools

**Buying behavior**:
- Willing to pay for professional tool + support
- High LTV if solved
- Wants customization + reporting

## Marketing Messaging

### Core Message

**"Uncover thousands in hidden AI spending in 2 minutes."**

Why this works:
- Benefit + speed (two most important attributes)
- Specific (not "improve your budget", but "uncover hidden AI spending")
- Credible (2 minutes is realistic)

### Supporting Messages

1. **"No signup. No email required. Start immediately."**
   - Addresses friction objection
   - Privacy signal

2. **"Share audit results with your finance team in seconds."**
   - Enables internal sharing without login
   - Supports decision-making

3. **"Get confidence scores on every recommendation."**
   - Differentiates from generic tools
   - Builds trust

### Value Propositions by Segment

**Finance Teams**:
- ✅ See total AI spend in one view
- ✅ Identify quick cost reduction wins
- ✅ Share findings with leadership confidently

**Engineering Teams**:
- ✅ See entire engineering AI tool portfolio
- ✅ Identify redundancy + consolidation opportunities
- ✅ Make data-driven tooling decisions

**Security/Compliance**:
- ✅ Inventory of all AI tools in use
- ✅ Access control overview
- ✅ Compliance documentation

## User Feedback Loop

### Gathering Feedback

1. **Post-audit survey** (1 question)
   - "Was this audit helpful?"
   - Yes/No → email to customer success

2. **Email to leads** (24 hours after audit)
   - "Thanks for using AI Spend Audit!"
   - "How can we improve? Reply to this email"

3. **Slack group** (community)
   - Finance leaders + tech leads
   - Share tips, ask questions, give feedback

### Feedback Analysis

Monthly:
- NPS score (target: >50)
- Feature requests (top 5)
- Support issues (zero critical)
- Recommendation accuracy (users acting on advice)

## Pricing Strategy

### Phase 1: Free (Launch → Month 3)
- 100% free to build adoption
- Goal: 5K audits, validate product-market fit

### Phase 2: Freemium (Month 4 →)
- Free: 1 audit per session
- Premium: $9/month (history + alerts)
- Goal: Convert 3–5% of free users

### Phase 3: Upgrade Premium → Pro ($29/month)
- When users ask for team collaboration
- When they want advanced forecasting
- Upsell after 30 days of premium usage

### Phase 4: Enterprise (Month 9+)
- Custom pricing ($500–$5K/month)
- API access + integrations
- Sales-driven process

## Competitive Strategy

### vs. Spreadsheet Audit
- **Beat on**: Speed + accuracy
- **Messaging**: "From hours to seconds"

### vs. Procurement SaaS
- **Beat on**: Price + simplicity + AI focus
- **Messaging**: "Procurement software for AI tools"

### vs. Custom Build
- **Beat on**: Speed to value + maintenance
- **Messaging**: "Don't build this yourself"

### vs. Future Competition
- **Defensibility**: Network effects (benchmark data), product quality, customer loyalty
- **Response**: Integrate + acquire if threatened
